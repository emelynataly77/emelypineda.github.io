Personal Background

(I promise I'm not conceited)

Some background on me: I was raised in Dallas, TX, and I’m now living in Houston (it is so humid here) after graduating college in 2024. I studied Biomedical Sciences and quickly fell in love with research. At first, I planned to go to vet school — I’m a huge animal lover and have the cutest dog named Bunny (I’ll insert a picture below…you’re welcome). Yes, he’s a dog. No, he’s not a bunny. But after taking a few courses in animal medicine, I realized the veterinary path wasn’t quite right for me.

After graduation, I began working at UTHealth, in a neurology lab researching protein aggregation and its effects on patients with neurodegenerative disorders. I won’t bore you with the science jargon (unless you’re into that, in which case, let’s talk), but it was during this time that I discovered a genuine passion for data analysis. I dive deeper into that in the Professional Background section — check it out if you haven’t yet.

Outside of work, I love to bake. People say baking is relaxing, I say it’s an experiment that sometimes ends with me covered in flour at 2 a.m., perfecting a sourdough recipe (still, I love it). I also recently got into chess and love to play during my free time (and contrary to what my partner says, I’m not a sore loser). When I’m not baking, playing chess, reading, or spending time with friends and family, I’m usually trying to learn something new. Whether it’s a scientific concept, a skill, or a hobby, I really believe the pursuit of knowledge is one of the most meaningful things we can do.

That belief is what led me to create this website, a place to share my work, my growth, and the journey I’m on as I continue exploring what I love. Thanks for stopping by.
