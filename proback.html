Professional Background

My journey through research and data analysis

I graduated from Texas A&M University, Class of 2024, where I discovered my love for laboratory research. After graduation, I joined UTHealth McGovern Medical School as a Research Assistant. I worked in a neurology lab focused on protein aggregation and its role in neurodegenerative diseases such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s. Even though I still cared about the research, I started to realize I was most excited about working with the data. I enjoyed diving into the results, spotting trends, and using those insights to help determine future experiments.

That interest pushed me to explore data and tech more seriously, so I decided to take the Google Data Analytics course. I got hands-on experience with tools like R, SQL, Tableau, and Excel. I completed the course and got my certificate, but I’m still learning every day and continuing to grow my skills. It became clear that I needed to shift toward a career where I could combine my interest in people, problem-solving, and data-driven decisions. I’m looking for a role where I can grow with a team that values collaboration, purpose, and curiosity.

Now you’re up up to date. I hope to hear from you soon!

If you'd like to learn more about me, check out my Data Analytics Certificate or My Time As A Scientist.
