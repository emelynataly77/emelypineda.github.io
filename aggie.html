My Time As An Aggie

Biomedical Sciences Major

After high school, I took a gap year to figure out where I wanted to go to college. After researching my options, I finally settled on Texas A&M. It turned out to be one of the best decisions of my life. My time as an Aggie was incredibly formative, shaping who I am today.

I quickly discovered an interest in research and data during my sophomore year while taking various lab and math courses. I was constantly collaborating with peers on projects and presentations, which helped me understand the importance of teamwork and clear communication. Beyond academics, I got involved in several programs during my college experience. I participated in a peer mentorship program, taking on a leadership role that helped underclassmen transition smoothly into college life academically and in general. I also volunteered as a peer notetaker for students with disabilities. These experiences really helped me develop leadership qualities and a sense of community amongst my peers. Of course, college wasn’t all work, I made some of my closest friends from group projects, and at A&M football and hockey games.

As a first-generation college student, I truly appreciated the opportunities that education could open. My time as an Aggie taught me how to manage my time, live away form home, and handle high levels of stress (especially during finals). Looking back, I can see how much these experiences laid the foundation for both my personal growth and my career development. I will alway cherich my time here.

College best friend and roommate.

Once an Aggie, always an Aggie.

And that’s my Aggie origin story. Full of learning, laughter, too much coffee, and the occasional friendship with a campus squirrel.
